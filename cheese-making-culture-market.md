<p><p>Cheese making culture market types include Mesophilic and Thermophilic cultures. Mesophilic cultures thrive at moderate temperatures, producing cheeses like cheddar and feta, while Thermophilic cultures are used in hot temperatures to make cheeses like mozzarella and gouda. Probiotics are another market type that contain live bacteria beneficial for gut health and are added to some cheeses to enhance their nutritional value. Each of these market types cater to different cheese making processes and end products.</p></p>
