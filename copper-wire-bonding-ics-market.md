<p><p>Copper wire bonding is a common technique used in integrated circuits (ICs) packaging. There are three main types of bonding methods: Ball-Ball Bonds, Wedge-Wedge Bonds, and Ball-Wedge Bonds. Ball-Ball Bonds involve attaching the wire to the IC using spherical connections, while Wedge-Wedge Bonds use angled wedges for the attachment. Ball-Wedge Bonds combine both spherical and wedge connections for a hybrid approach. Each bonding method offers unique advantages and is selected based on specific requirements of the IC design and application.</p></p>
