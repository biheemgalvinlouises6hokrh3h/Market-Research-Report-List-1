<p><p>Bicycle-sharing systems can be categorized into three main market types: Point-to-Point, Ride-and-Return, and Distributed markets. </p><p>Point-to-Point systems allow users to pick up a bike at one location and drop it off at another, offering convenience and flexibility for users. </p><p>Ride-and-Return systems require users to return the bike to the same location where they picked it up, ensuring availability at specific docking stations. </p><p>Distributed markets operate without any fixed docking stations, allowing users to pick up and drop off bikes at various locations throughout the city.</p></p>
