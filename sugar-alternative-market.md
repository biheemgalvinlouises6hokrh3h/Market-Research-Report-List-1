<p><p>The sugar alternative market consists of three main types: High Fructose Syrup, High Intensity Sweeteners, and Low Intensity Sweeteners. High Fructose Syrup, such as corn syrup, is used in a wide range of processed foods and beverages. High Intensity Sweeteners, like aspartame and sucralose, are much sweeter than sugar and are used in smaller quantities in products like diet sodas. Low Intensity Sweeteners, such as stevia and monk fruit extract, provide sweetness with fewer calories and are often used in natural or low-calorie products.</p></p>
