<p><p>Low-light imaging technology utilizes sensors such as Charge Coupled Devices (CCD) and Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor (CMOS) transistors. CCD sensors are known for their high-quality image output and low noise levels, making them ideal for professional photography and scientific imaging. On the other hand, CMOS sensors are more cost-effective and energy-efficient, making them popular for consumer electronics like smartphones and security cameras. Both technologies play a crucial role in improving image quality in low-light conditions across various markets.</p></p>
