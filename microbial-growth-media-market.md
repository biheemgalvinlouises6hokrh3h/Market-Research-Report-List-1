<p><p>Microbial growth media can be categorized into different types based on their composition and complexity. Simple media are basic formulations containing essential nutrients for the growth of microorganisms. Complex media contain more complex nutrients such as yeast extract or peptone. Synthetic media are precisely defined and contain specific components in known quantities. Special media are tailored for the growth of specific microbial species, such as selective media for the isolation of certain bacteria. Each type of media serves a specific purpose in supporting microbial growth.</p></p>
