<p><p>Microencapsulation and nanoencapsulation are both types of encapsulation technologies used in various industries such as food, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and textiles. </p><p>Microencapsulation involves enclosing tiny particles or droplets of active ingredients within a shell, typically ranging in size from 1-1000 micrometers. </p><p>On the other hand, nanoencapsulation involves encapsulating materials at nanoscale, typically less than 100 nanometers in size. </p><p>Both these technologies offer benefits such as improved stability, protection from degradation, controlled release of ingredients, and improved bioavailability.</p></p>
