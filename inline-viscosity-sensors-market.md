<p><p>Inline viscosity sensors are used in various industries to measure the viscosity (thickness or consistency) of liquids. They are categorized into low-temperature and high-temperature sensors. Low-temperature sensors are designed to operate in cold environments, such as refrigeration units or cryogenic applications, where viscosity measurements are required at below-normal temperatures. On the other hand, high-temperature sensors are suitable for extreme heat conditions, like molten metals or industrial processing, where viscosity measurements are needed at elevated temperatures. Both types of sensors enable precise and reliable viscosity control in their respective operating environments.</p></p>
